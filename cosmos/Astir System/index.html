    The Astir System is a P-type binary solar system, with two suns: Astir I, a bright G6 star, and Astir II, a dim K4 star. They have an average separation of \(\pu{0.17}{AU}\), a minimum separation of 0.10 AU, and a maximum separation of 0.24 AU.

    Class Mass Radius Luminosity
    Astir I G6 0.92 M☉ 0.72 R☉ 0.72 L☉
    Astir II K4 0.66 M☉ 0.19 R☉ 0.19 L☉
    Total 1.58 M☉ 1.66 R☉ 0.91 L☉

    The Astir System extends from 0.16 AU to 63.20 AU from the barycenter, with its frost line at 4.63 AU. The habitable zone reaches from 0.91 AU to 1.31 AU. Finally, the unstable region extends from 0.03 AU to 0.72 AU. Habitable planets must be at least 0.95 AU from the barycenter.

    The Astir System exists in an incredibly precarious balance, just inches away from collapsing inwards on itself. Perhaps this is evidence of a higher power—or simply evidence of an advanced civilization with godlike technology, long gone.
